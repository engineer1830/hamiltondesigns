# Discipleship: Coming Unto Christ  
## Faith, Works, and the Grace That Bridges the Gap

Discipleship is the lifelong process of **coming unto Christ**. It involves faith, works, and a deep recognition of our absolute dependence on the Savior’s grace.

## Faith and Works — Where Does Grace Fit?

We choose faith, and our works naturally flow from that faith.  
But while we are busy “working out our salvation,” we must recognize a humbling truth:

**No amount of works — no matter how extraordinary — can bridge the gap between us and God.**

Only Jesus Christ can do that.

Discipleship requires coming to the realization that we are utterly dependent on Him.  
It is the surrender of our will, the offering of our whole soul.

As we mature spiritually, we begin to understand that **our will is the only thing we truly have to offer** our Heavenly Father. Everything else is already His.

And through lived experience, we eventually discover:

- We are not in control of the events of our lives.  
- We have never been in control of the events of our lives.

## Aligning Our Will With God’s Will

Coming unto Christ means aligning our will with the will of our Father in Heaven.  
It is choosing to trust that He knows what is best for us.

We still:

- prayerfully choose directions  
- pursue righteous desires  
- engage in good causes  
- work diligently  

…but we do so with a heart willing to be guided, corrected, and redirected.
