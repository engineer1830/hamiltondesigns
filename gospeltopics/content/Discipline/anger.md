# Anger

Anger shows up throughout the scriptures, but not all anger is the same.  
Some anger leads to righteousness, protection, and spiritual strength.  
Other anger leads to pride, destruction, and bondage.  
The contrast between Captain Moroni and Amalickiah illustrates this difference with clarity.

## Righteous Anger: Captain Moroni

**Alma 46:11** — Moroni was angry with Amalickiah.  
But his anger came from a place of **love**, **covenant loyalty**, and **deep concern** for his people.

Moroni’s anger:

- Came because he loved his people and sought their welfare.
- Stirred his people up to **righteousness**, not revenge.
- Motivated him to **prepare their minds to be faithful unto the Lord** (Alma 48:7).
- Led him to **pray mightily unto God** (Alma 46:13).
- Caused him to plead that the cause of the Christians would be favored (Alma 46:16).
- Moved him to pour out his whole soul so his people would not be destroyed (Alma 46:17–18).

Moroni’s anger aligned with **principles**, **covenants**, and **doctrine**.  
It turned people **toward Christ**, not away from Him.

## Destructive Anger: Amalickiah

**Alma 46:4–10** — Amalickiah sought his own glory.  
His anger was rooted in pride, ambition, and a desire for power.

**Alma 47:1** — He stirred up his people to anger to bring the Nephites into bondage or destroy them.

Amalickiah’s anger:

- Was fueled by pride and personal ambition.
- Incited others to violence and hatred.
- Sought revenge for imagined offenses.
- Had no principles, no doctrine, and no regard for God.
- Led to bondage, destruction, and spiritual decay.

His anger was **self‑centered**, **manipulative**, and **destructive**.

## Two Very Different Outcomes

Moroni’s anger → **Faith, preparation, prayer, protection, righteousness**  
Amalickiah’s anger → **Bondage, manipulation, pride, destruction**

The scriptures show that anger itself is not the issue.  
The **source**, **purpose**, and **direction** of anger determine whether it becomes a tool for righteousness or a weapon of destruction.
